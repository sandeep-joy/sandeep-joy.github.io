I'm Sandeep Joy, a graduate student at the Ohio State University, working with Dr. [Brian Skinner](https://sites.google.com/view/skinner-physics) in the Physics Department. My research focuses on theoretical condensed matter physics, particularly the exploration of quantum materials.

My primary research interests revolve around the exploration of quantum materials. During my graduate studies, I primarily focused on investigating Wigner crystal phases and phase coexistence in two-dimensional electron systems. We developed a theory of Wigner crystallization at arbitrary values of the effective fine structure constant. Our most recent work showed that the interplay between the "Mexican-hat" band structure and Berry curvature can induce novel phase transitions within the Wigner crystal phase of Bernal bilayer graphene. In a related but distinct line of research, we established an upper bound on the density range over which "microemulsions" (mesoscopic mixing near putative first-order transitions) may exist. Furthermore, our findings contributed to collaborative work with the University of California, Santa Barbara experimental group, focusing on the transition between stripe and bubble phases of graphene electrons in a magnetic field. In the earlier stages of my graduate studies, I developed the theory of electron transport in the presence of twist-angle disorder in twisted bilayer graphene.
